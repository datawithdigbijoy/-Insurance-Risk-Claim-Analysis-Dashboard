# Insurance-Risk-Claim-Analysis-Dashboard

## Project Title

This project presents an analytical dashboard focused on understanding insurance claim behavior based on demographic, vehicle, and regional factors. The goal is to identify patterns that influence claim amounts, frequency, risk segmentation, and customer profiles to support better decision-making for underwriting and pricing strategies.

![insurence dashboard](https://github.com/datawithdigbijoy/-Insurance-Risk-Claim-Analysis-Dashboard/blob/main/insurance.jpg)

**Objective**

To analyse insurance claim data and extract key trends that support better risk management, profitability, and customer segmentation.

**Tools & Techniques Used**

- Power BI – Data Modelling, DAX Measures, Interactive Visuals
- Power Query – Data Cleaning & Transformation
- Visuals – Donut Charts, Bar Graphs, Line Charts, Cards & Filters

**Key Highlight**

- Total Policies: 37,542
- Total Claim Amount: $187.8M
- Average Claim Amount: $5.0K
- Gender Split: Male (18.7K) | Female (18.8K)

**Insights by Segment**

- Car Use: Private vehicles account for most claim value ($150.4M), while commercial claims total $37.4M.
- Car Make: Ford leads with $17M in claim amount, followed by Chevrolet ($15M).
- Age Group: Highest claims occur from 46–55 and 36–45 age segments (~$35M–$36M).
- Claims Over Time: Significant spike around 2000–2010, peaking near $9.1M.
- Coverage Zone: Urban area contributes the largest share (20.35%).
- Kids Driving: Highest risk area with $134M in claims at driving year 0.
- Education Level: Bachelor's degree leads with $94M (50.06%).

**Business Value:**
*This analysis helps*
- Identify high-risk customer groups
- Support pricing & underwriting policies
- Reduce claim losses through targeted controls
- Improve customer segmentation
